Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Overview

PalmOracle is an iOS app that provides AI-powered palmistry readings based on photographs of your palms. This policy explains what information the app collects, how it is used, and what is never collected.

Developer: Ranidu Lankage

PalmOracle is designed with privacy at its core. There are no user accounts, no sign-ups, and no personal profiles. Your readings belong to you and stay on your device.

What We Collect

PalmOracle collects only the minimum information necessary to function:

  • Palm images — Photos you take or select are sent to our AI backend solely to generate your reading. Images are processed in transit and are not stored on any server after the reading is complete.
  • Anonymous device ID — A random identifier (never tied to your identity, Apple ID, or any personal information) is used exclusively to enforce the free reading limit and prevent abuse. It is not linked to any other data.
  • Anonymous usage analytics — Basic events such as "reading started" and "reading completed" are logged to help us understand how the app is used and improve the experience. These events contain no personal data.

What We Do Not Collect

PalmOracle does not collect, store, or process any of the following:

Name Email address Phone number Location data Apple ID Contacts Health data Financial information Browsing history Precise device identifiers Biometric data Account credentials

Reading History & Local Storage

All reading history — including palm images, the full reading text, and follow-up chat messages — is stored locally on your device only. This data never leaves your device except when you explicitly use the Share or Export features.

Your reading history is automatically included in your device's standard iCloud Backup (if you have iCloud Backup enabled) as part of normal iOS backup behavior. This is controlled entirely by your own iCloud settings.

When you delete the PalmOracle app, all locally stored readings and data are permanently removed from your device.

Third-Party Services

PalmOracle uses the following third-party services to deliver its functionality:

Anthropic Claude API

Palm images and reading requests are processed by Anthropic's Claude AI model via a secure proxy server. Images are transmitted over an encrypted connection (HTTPS) and are not retained after the reading is generated. Anthropic's privacy policy is available at anthropic.com/privacy.

Supabase

Supabase powers our backend proxy, which handles AI request routing, anonymous usage analytics events, and rate limiting. No personally identifiable information is stored in Supabase. Supabase's privacy policy is available at supabase.com/privacy.

The Anthropic API key is held exclusively on the server and is never stored on or transmitted to your device.

Data Security

All network communication between PalmOracle and our backend uses HTTPS with TLS encryption. Locally stored readings are protected by iOS Data Protection (NSFileProtectionCompleteUnlessOpen), meaning they are encrypted at rest and inaccessible while the device is locked.

Because there are no user accounts, there are no passwords to compromise and no central database of user profiles to breach.

Children's Privacy

PalmOracle is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from children. If you believe a child has used the app, please contact us using the address below.
